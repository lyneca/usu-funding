USU Funding Changes

As of Febuary 2019, the USU has changed student pricing for ACCESS membership:

  • $0 membership fee
  • optional $45 'discount' price (for a 10% discount campus-wide)

This means that societies will likely see more registered members attending their events, with more students interested and able to join societies and less unregistered students turning up to events.

However, they have also cut funding for society events. Previously, societies were funded for $6 per ACCESS member for on-campus events, or $4 for off-campus events, up until breaking even. This is represented by the blue line on the graph to the right.

With the new funding scheme, societies are funded for 30% 50% of the total event cost for on-campus events (off-campus events stay at 30%), plus $1 per USU member attending. This is represented by the red line.

The USU also has the option to apply for a large grant of money for camps, and another yearly grant for large events.

The USU's Strategic Plan 2017-2020, and past annual reports (including financial reports) can be downloaded here.

Update 15/07/2019: As of the 5th of August 2019, on-campus events have had their funding increased from 30% to 50%.
